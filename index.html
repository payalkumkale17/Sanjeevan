What Sanjeevan does

Six tools. One app.

Everything you need to understand the air around you, without switching between sources.

Live AQI dashboard

Current AQI, PM2.5, PM10, temperature, humidity, and wind speed — from OpenAQ and Open-Meteo, refreshed in real time for your selected city.

Global city rankings

Compare your city against 28 cities worldwide, sortable by AQI or PM2.5, with 24-hour trend indicators and colour-coded status badges.

72-hour forecast

A rule-based forecast model using wind, humidity, and temperature to project how air quality will evolve over the next three days.

Pollution origins

Understand what is driving today's AQI. Vehicles, dust, industry, and burning — each source quantified with its estimated AQI contribution.

Personalised guidance

Health recommendations adapted to your profile — condition, age, activity pattern, and daily outdoor hours — so advice is relevant to you specifically.

Adaptive dark mode

System-wide theming via React Context. AQI colour semantics stay consistent across both themes — a safe reading is always green, a hazardous one always maroon.

Dashboard

Reference standards

Built on recognised science

Sanjeevan's colour coding follows the US EPA AQI scale. Here is how that relates to Indian and WHO thresholds.

US EPA AQI — used in app

12
µg/m³ PM2.5 — "Good" ceiling (24-hr mean)

The six-tier colour scale used across Sanjeevan's dashboard, badges, and rankings. Chosen for global familiarity and consistent API support from OpenAQ.

India NAQI — CPCB

30
µg/m³ PM2.5 — "Good" ceiling (24-hr mean)

India's national standard uses broader bands calibrated to local ambient conditions. Breakpoints differ from the US EPA scale — direct comparison requires care.

WHO 2021 Guideline

5
µg/m³ PM2.5 — annual mean limit

Far stricter than either national index. Many Indian cities classified as "Moderate" by US EPA or NAQI still exceed the WHO guideline significantly.
